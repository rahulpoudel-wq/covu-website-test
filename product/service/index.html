Who runs it

Who runs your requests.

Every request is routed to the right kind of help for what it actually needs. Licensed work goes to licensed agents. The repeatable prep gets handled without tying up your most expensive people. You get the capacity when the work is there, and it steps back when it is not.

Licensed

Licensed agents

Handle the work that legally requires a license, and sign off on it.

  • Commercial and personal lines (CL and PL) agents
  • 8+ years of experience
  • U.S.-based
  • Work reviewed and signed off, not handed back unchecked
Unlicensed

Unlicensed professionals

Clear everything around the licensed work, so your licensed staff are not buried in prep.

  • Document collection, data entry, and request prep
  • Keep your licensed people free for the work only they can do
AI, routed

VERO AI

Takes the routine, repeatable parts of a request off the queue first.

  • Runs the high-volume, repeatable prep
  • License-gated, so it only does what does not require a license
  • Every action logged, with an audit trail

The COVU Service Network is a measured capacity layer made up of U.S.-based licensed agents, trained insurance professionals, and COVU's proprietary AI, Vero.

Regulated work should go to licensed people. Routine non-licensed tasks can be routed to trained capacity or AI. For example, gathering information from a carrier when there is no IVANS access may not require a license, even if the full service request ultimately needs licensed review or completion.

Work is routed through COVU OS with license-aware rules, QA, and an audit trail on each handoff.

Yes. The Service Network is extra capacity when you need it, not a permanent replacement for your staff.
